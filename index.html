Spring 2025, M/W 11.40-12.55 PSAM3707 Syllabus

Course Overview: Understanding Persuasion in the Digital Age

Key Topics Covered

This cross-disciplinary course explores the multifaceted nature of persuasion in the modern world, bridging political science, communication, journalism, marketing, and public health. The course combines rich theories of communication with practical, hands-on analysis.

Students will engage with real-world data using statistical and computational methods, including:

  • Randomized controlled trials
  • Natural experiments
  • Machine learning techniques
  • Causal inference methods

This course fulfills the quantitative methods requirement for the Political Science major.

Course Details

  • Call Number: 17626

By employing statistical and computational methods, including randomized controlled trials, natural experiments, and machine learning techniques, students will engage directly with real-world data to uncover the intricacies of persuasion across different sectors, including but not limited to quantifying the effects of partisan media, social media, and political campaigns. The course will also delve into the historical evolution of these persuasive techniques, providing students with a rich contextual background to better understand current trends and anticipate future developments.

Course Requirements

Grading Breakdown

  • Participation: 10% - Active engagement in #discussion channel on Slack, with weekly posts and in-class elaboration.
  • Homework Assignments: 60% (20% each)
    • Media Consumption Analysis: Compare self-reported media habits with behavioral data to uncover discrepancies.
    • Persuasion Experiment Design: Create and analyze a media experiment using simulated data.
    • BlueSky Data Exploration: Develop strategies to encourage engagement, minimize regret minutes, and reduce trolling.
  • Final Exam: 30% - In-class, pen-only evaluation of course concepts.

All assignments must be submitted to pass the course. Late work policies and extension rules apply as per the syllabus.
